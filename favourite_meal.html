Amala and Abula

Origin

Amala is made out of yam and/or cassava flour or unripe plantain flour. Tubers of yam are peeled, sliced, cleaned,dried and then blended into flour. It is also called elubo. Yams are white in color but turn brown when dried which gives the amala its color

Abula is a soup of the Yoruba peaople from Western Nigerian. It is typically eaten together with Amala, but can be eaten with other swallow foods. Abula literally means a mixture of soups, but it is mostly associated to gbegiri(beans soup), Ewedu(draw vegetable soup) and obe ata(stew).
Gbegiri is a traditional Nigerian bean soup consisting of honey beans (also called brown beans), smoked or fresh fish, ground crayfish, palm oil, stock, ir√∫ (locust beans), salt, and pepper. The beans are soaked, peeled, and mashed to a smooth paste, which is then combined with the other ingredients and cooked until the mixture reaches a slightly thickened consistency.

Ewedu soup is a soup created by the Yoruba ethnic group similar to Okra soup, it is made from jute leaf, hence it is known as jute leaf soup. The soup is drawy in texture and goes with Nigerian beef stew and fish stew.

Obe ata is a West African tomato and pepper soup that's absolutely unmatched in its versality. It's a dish that has been embraced by both home cooks and as well as professional chefs in West Africa cooking. It is often eaten with other soups, paired with various swallows.

Amala
Ingredients Measurements
Water 4 cups
Yam flour-sifted 2 cups
Hot water(reserved) 1 cup

Preparation:
  • Boil water in a pot and when it start making bubbles, pour in the yam flour
  • Keep turning until there is a consistency in the paste
  • Add the reserved hot water and make holes in the paste to the water can perforate deep to the base of the pot
  • Cover for like 5-6 minutes then turn the paste again till smooth
  • Your amala is now ready!

Gbegiri
Ingredients Measurements
Brown beans, peeled 1-2 cups
Palm oil 1 cup
Crayfish powder 2 tbs
Locust beans(iru) 1 tbs
Ground pepper(atagungun) 1-2 tbs
Seasoning to taste
Salt to taste

Preparation:
  • Steps on getting the beans ready.
    • Soak then peel your beans
    • Add peeled beans into a pot
    • Place a hob on high heat
    • Add water and cook till very soft and mushy
    • Be careful not to add much water
    • You can start with 2 cups and add as you go
    • Do not add salt
    • When soft, use ijabe(ewedu broom) to mash up the beans or whisk. You can also use a blender for this
    • Add water as you go, say half a cup
  • Now that the paste is ready, return it back to the pot and on low heat, add water if paste is too thick.
  • Add palm oil, iru, crayfish, Seasoning and salt to taste.
  • Be careful not to add too much palm oil, it's only necessary for the color change of paste.
  • Cook for 5-7 minutes, stir ocassionally to prevent burning.
  • Its ready when the gbegiri soup thickens a bit and the palm oil is well combined with the paste


Ewedu
Ingredients Measurements
Ewedu leaves 1 big bunch
Water 2 cups
Locust beans 1 tbs
Salt to taste

Preparation:
  • Pick the jute leaves from the stalk and wash with warm water to get rid of the dirt.
  • Pour the ewedu leaves in a boiling water
  • Add locust beans and salt
  • Use ijabe to mash the leaves when soft or pour in a blender
  • Your green, slimy ewedu is now ready!


Obe Ata
Ingredients Measurements
Red bell pepper 2pieces, about 2kg
Green bell pepper 2 large green pieces
Scotch bonnet pepper 2 pieces
Onion 1 large piece
Chopped onions 1 large piece
Red oil 9 tbs
Tomatoes 3 large pieces
Seasoning to taste
Salt to taste

Preparation:
  • Blend the green,red pepper,onion,tomatoes,
  • Pour the mixture into a medium pot set on high heat. Cook for 20mins to remove excess water.
  • Set a large pot on medium heat, add in half of the palm oil, bleach for about 2-5mins, add in the chopped onions
  • Add in the parboiled pureed mixture, half-1 cup of water(depending on how you like your stew)
  • Add seasoning and salt.
  • Simmer for 15minutes
  • Reduce the heat to below medium, add in precooked meat. Taste and adjust for seasoning.
  • Cover and simmer for another 10mins. Add in the leftover palmoil
  • The soup is ready when the oil floats to the top.
